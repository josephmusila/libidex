Hypertension is often referred to as a "silent killer" because it may not show noticeable symptoms until it reaches an advanced stage. Regular blood pressure monitoring is crucial for early detection.

The World Health Organization (WHO) estimated that around 1.13 billion people worldwide have hypertension

Hypertension contributes significantly to the global burden of disease and is a leading cause of premature death and disability

Watch Out For signs like
  • Headaches: Persistent headaches, especially at the back of the head, can be a symptom of hypertension.
  • Shortness of Breath: Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath, even with mild physical activity, may be a sign of elevated blood pressure.
  • Nosebleeds: While nosebleeds can have various causes, they are sometimes associated with hypertension.
  • Dizziness or Lightheadedness: Feeling dizzy or lightheaded, particularly when standing up quickly, can be a symptom.
  • Vision Problems: Blurred or double vision may occur in some individuals with high blood pressure.
  • Chest Pain: Chest pain or discomfort may be a symptom, especially during physical exertion.
  • Fatigue or Confusion: Persistent fatigue, feeling tired, or confusion can sometimes be linked to high blood pressure.
